whats a real highest convertion landing page must have?

There are several key elements that are often found on high-converting landing pages. These elements include:

1. A clear and compelling value proposition: This is a statement that explains the benefits of your product or service and why the visitor should take action.

2. A strong and relevant headline: The headline is the first thing visitors see when they arrive at your landing page, so it's important to make it attention-grabbing and relevant to the visitor's needs.

3. Supporting subheadings and bullet points: These can help to clarify and expand upon the value proposition, and can make it easier for visitors to scan and understand the main points.

4. Social proof: This could include customer testimonials, ratings and reviews, or logos of well-known companies that use your product. Social proof helps to build trust and credibility.

5. A prominent and compelling call-to-action: This is the button or link that visitors click to take the desired action, such as filling out a form or making a purchase. The call-to-action should be clear and easy to find.

6. Trust indicators: These could include security badges, money-back guarantees, or third-party certifications, and help to build trust and credibility with visitors.

7. Responsive design: With more and more people accessing the internet on their phones, it's important to ensure that your landing page is mobile-friendly and easy to use on smaller screens.

whats is the importants points of the book influence by robert Cialdini

"Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion" is a classic book on the psychology of persuasion and has been highly influential in the fields of marketing, advertising, and psychology. The book identifies six key principles of persuasion, which are:

1. Reciprocation: People tend to feel obligated to give back to others who have given to them.

2. Commitment and consistency: Once people make a commitment, they are more likely to follow through with it.

3. Social proof: People are more likely to do something if they see others doing it.

4. Authority: People are more likely to follow the advice of an authority figure.

5. Liking: People are more likely to comply with requests from those they like.

6. Scarcity: People perceive things as more valuable when they are scarce or in short supply.

The book also discusses how these principles can be used ethically and unethically, and provides practical tips for applying these principles to influence others.

5. Highlight the benefits of owning the product: By focusing on the unique benefits and value of the product, you can create a sense of scarcity by highlighting what people will miss out on if they don't take advantage of the opportunity.
